Types of Reasoning

  1. Deductive Reasoning
    1. Also known as the Socratic method and used by lawyers. We use a chain of logic to prove conclusions based on given facts, often using if...then statements
  2. Inductive Reasoning
    1. Used in science and getting to the truth. We look at specific examples made through primary or secondary observations and draw conclusions from them. Examples include Gravitational and Evolutionary Theory. We look for counterexamples. If no counter examples are found, we accept the theory as the truth.

Anectodal Evidence

An anecdote is a single story or example. It is the worst form of evidence because one example doesn't prove anything. It cannot be used to extrapolate out to a larger population any kind of predictable theory. Anecdotes can be used for illustrative purposes or to get people interested in the topic. However, it isn't good evidence.

Statistical Evidence

This is the best form of evidence, so long as certain methods are followed.

  1. Subjects need to be selected at random from the general population of those being studied. Volunteers for "treatments" are biased.
  2. Subjects and those administering the treatment need to be blind as to whom is receiving the treatment. Double-blind.
  3. Subjects need to come from a large sample size.
  4. Half of the subjects should be treated and the other half treated with a placebo.
  5. If statistics come from survey questions, survey questions should not lead the subject.

Presenting statistics

  1. Statistics should be presented as both raw numbers and ratios or percentages.

  2. Raw numbers provide a sense of scale.
  3. Percentages and ratios provide context: 70% of America's debt is owed to itself.
  4. A percentage is a number out of 100: 48% of people surveyed, 23% of all students.
  5. A ratio is a number out of another number: 3 out of 5 dentists, 1 in 10 babies.

Quotations

Quotations provide evidence when they come from actual experts and not self-appointed experts. So, who is an expert?

  1. Experts are people who have 10,000 hours of effortful practice in their field.
  2. Experts have proven their expertise through practice or publishing in a peer-reviewed journal
