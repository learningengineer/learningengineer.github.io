Outlines

What is an outline?

An outline is the use of written words to provide a condensed version of a longer piece of writing with the summarization of details. According to the APA Style Guide, writing from an outline is the most important method of creating quality writing.

Kaplan's Bar Review channel at Youtube does an excellent job explaining outlines and how to use them. This video may be about writing outlines for law school material; however, you can simply replace law school with whatever field you are in and the details that are important to that field.

How an outline works in an essay.

    Three Steps to Good Structure

  1. Create a thesis statement.
  2. Develop and outline the argument of your paper.
  3. When revising your paper ask yourself, “How does this paragraph help me prove my thesis?”

    Step One: Writing a Thesis Statement

  1. Think of your thesis statement as the goal of your paper.
  2. Try to state the goal of your paper in a single sentence.

    Example Thesis Statements

  1. For an article review:
    1. A thorough summary and evaluation of Dawkins' article demonstrates that Dawkins was successful in proving that the moon landings were not faked.
    2. This is a good, clear thesis statement and it also shows the two main components of the body of the paper: a summary and evaluation.
  2. For a research paper:
    1. The evidence clearly demonstrates that the moon landings were not faked.
  3. For a reflection or reaction paper:
    1. These experiences have made a tremendous impact in both my spiritual and emotional life.
    2. This thesis statement also has built in structure. This statement dictates that there should be two main points to the paper: spiritual influence and emotional influence.
